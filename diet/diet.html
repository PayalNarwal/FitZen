Curated Diet Plans for Every Goal

Bulking

Bulking is focused on gaining muscle mass. Aim for a caloric surplus, with increased protein intake to support muscle growth.

Sample Indian Diet Plan (4 meals):

  • Breakfast: 3-4 whole eggs, 2 slices of whole-grain bread, and a banana.
  • Lunch: 1 cup of brown rice, 150g chicken or paneer, and mixed vegetables.
  • Snack: 1 cup of yogurt with nuts and honey.
  • Dinner: 1-2 whole wheat chapatis, 150g fish or tofu, and a side salad.

Cutting

Cutting focuses on reducing body fat while preserving muscle. Maintain a caloric deficit with high protein intake.

Sample Indian Diet Plan (4 meals):

  • Breakfast: Oatmeal with skim milk and fruits.
  • Lunch: 1 cup of quinoa, grilled chicken or chickpeas, and steamed broccoli.
  • Snack: 1 apple with peanut butter.
  • Dinner: Grilled vegetables, 2 whole wheat chapatis, and a bowl of dal.

Healthy Life

A healthy lifestyle focuses on balanced nutrition and regular exercise. The goal is to maintain overall well-being.

Sample Indian Diet Plan (4 meals):

  • Breakfast: Smoothie with spinach, banana, and almond milk.
  • Lunch: Mixed vegetable salad with chickpeas and a dressing of olive oil and lemon.
  • Snack: Whole-grain toast with avocado.
  • Dinner: 1 cup of brown rice, 150g grilled chicken or tofu, and stir-fried vegetables.

Essential Nutrients for a Balanced Diet

Proteins

Function: Building and repairing tissues, enzymes, hormones, and supporting muscle growth.

Foods: Chicken, fish, tofu, beans, eggs, lentils.

Carbohydrates

Function: Primary source of energy for the body.

Foods: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, quinoa, oats.

Fats

Function: Provides energy, supports cell growth, protects organs, and helps absorb vitamins.

Foods: Avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, fatty fish.

Vitamins

Function: Vital for immunity, skin health, and energy production.

Foods: Citrus fruits, leafy greens, dairy, eggs.

Minerals

Function: Essential for bone health, fluid balance, and muscle function.

Foods: Leafy greens, nuts, seeds, fish, dairy.

Water

Function: Crucial for hydration, temperature regulation, and digestion.

Foods: Water-rich fruits like cucumbers, watermelon, and oranges.

Fiber

Function: Supports digestion and helps regulate blood sugar levels.

Foods: Whole grains, beans, fruits, and vegetables.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Function: Supports heart health, brain function, and reduces inflammation.

Foods: Fatty fish, flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts.

Antioxidants

Function: Protects cells from damage by free radicals and supports overall health.

Foods: Berries, dark chocolate, nuts, spinach, broccoli.

Explore Different Types of Diets

Vegetarian Diet

A vegetarian diet excludes meat, fish, and poultry. It focuses on plant-based foods such as fruits, vegetables, grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds.

Vegan Diet

A vegan diet excludes all animal products, including meat, dairy, eggs, and honey, emphasizing whole foods like fruits, vegetables, and grains.

Non-Vegetarian Diet

A non-vegetarian diet includes the consumption of meat, poultry, and fish, providing a complete profile of essential nutrients.

Pescatarian Diet

A pescatarian diet includes fish and seafood while focusing on plant-based foods for protein and nutrition.

Keto Diet

The keto diet is high-fat and low-carb, putting the body into ketosis to burn fat for energy.

Mediterranean Diet

The Mediterranean diet focuses on whole foods, healthy fats, and lean proteins, emphasizing fruits, vegetables, nuts, and olive oil.

Paleo Diet

The Paleo diet is based on the presumed diet of Paleolithic humans, focusing on whole foods like lean meats, fish, fruits, and vegetables.

Whole30 Diet

The Whole30 diet involves eliminating sugar, grains, dairy, and legumes for 30 days to reset eating habits.
